iPhone vs Samsung: Which Should You Choose?

iPhone 15 Pro vs Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra - Complete Comparison (Australian Edition)

Apple: The Tech Giant Behind iPhone

Founded: 1 April 1976 in Cupertino, California by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne

First iPhone: Released 29 June 2007 - revolutionised smartphones with touchscreen and apps

Market Value: A$4.2 trillion (most valuable company in the world)

iPhone Sales: Over 230 million iPhones sold annually

Interesting Fact: The original iPhone development cost A$225 million and was kept secret even from most Apple employees

Samsung: From Food Export to Phone Giant

Founded: 1938 in South Korea as a trading company exporting dried fish and noodles

First Galaxy Phone: June 2009 (Galaxy i7500) to compete with iPhone

Market Value: A$555 billion

Galaxy Sales: Over 250 million smartphones sold annually

Interesting Fact: Samsung makes components used in iPhones, including screens and chips, while competing with Apple in phones

Apple iPhone 15 Pro (From A$1,849)

What's Good:

  • • Super smooth and easy to use
  • • Excellent cameras (especially for Instagram)
  • • All your Apple devices work together seamlessly
  • • Holds its value if you want to sell it later
  • • Receives updates for 5+ years

What's Not So Good:

  • • Expensive (starts at A$1,849)
  • • Doesn't come with a charger in the box
  • • Limited customisation options
  • • Battery life could be better

Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra (From A$2,199)

What's Good:

  • • Larger, brighter screen (great for videos)
  • • Includes S Pen for drawing and notes
  • • More camera options (200MP main camera)
  • • More customisation options than iPhone
  • • Better battery life

What's Not So Good:

  • • More expensive (starts at A$2,199)
  • • Large and heavy (may not fit small pockets)
  • • Doesn't integrate as well with Apple devices
  • • Receives updates for shorter period than iPhone

Which Phone Should You Choose?

Choose the iPhone 15 Pro if:

  • • You already own other Apple devices (Mac, iPad, AirPods)
  • • You prefer simplicity and ease of use
  • • You care about resale value
  • • Most of your contacts use iPhones (iMessage!)
  • • You prefer iOS over Android
  • • Budget: From A$1,849

Choose the Samsung S24 Ultra if:

  • • You want the most powerful Android phone available
  • • You enjoy customising your phone's appearance
  • • You'll use the S Pen for notes or drawing
  • • You prefer Android's flexibility
  • • You want the highest camera specs
  • • Budget: From A$2,199

Our Recommendation for Australian Buyers:

The iPhone 15 Pro is better for most people because:

  1. 1. It's A$350 cheaper than the S24 Ultra
  2. 2. Integrates perfectly with other Apple products
  3. 3. Receives updates for 5+ years (Samsung about 4 years)
  4. 4. Easier to get support at Apple Stores across Australia
  5. 5. Better resale value in the Australian market

But the S24 Ultra might be better if:

  • • You specifically want that large screen and S Pen functionality
  • • You prefer Android's customisation options
  • • You're a photography enthusiast wanting 200MP capability
  • • The larger size and weight don't bother you

Australian Buying Tips:

  • • Check for education discounts if you're a student or teacher
  • • Consider buying during end-of-financial-year sales (June-July)
  • • Compare prices across JB Hi-Fi, Harvey Norman, and official stores
  • • Remember all devices come with Australian charger plugs
